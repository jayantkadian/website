Oil Pipelines Hyderabad

Indian Oil Corporation Ltd. operates a network of about 12,848 km long crude oil, petroleum product and gas pipelines with a throughput capacity of 93.7 million metric tonnes per annum of oil and 9.5 million metric standard cubic meter per day of gas. Cross-country pipelines are globally recognised as the safest, cost-effective, energy-efficient and environment-friendly mode for transportation of crude oil and petroleum products.

Vijaywada

HPCL's 570-km long product pipeline from Visakhapatnam to Secunderabad has become operational since the middle of March. The pipeline will carry petrol, diesel, kerosene and naphtha from HPCL's Vizag refinery to different parts of Andhra Pradesh. In fact, the 350-km long stretch from Visakhapatnam to Vijayawada was commissioned in May 1998 with a designed capacity of 4 million tonnes. The extension of the pipeline up to Secunderabad involved an additional 223 km of pipeline with an intermediate tap-off-cum-booster station at Suryapet and the final receiving station at Ghatkesar on the outskirts of Hyderabad. Between Visakhapatnam and Vijayawada, there is a terminal at Rajamundry.
The movement of throughput between Visakhapatnam and Vijayawada has touched 3.20 million tonnes in the last fiscal. The movement through the entire stretch of Visakhapatnam-Secunderabad pipeline is expected to go up to 5.50 million tonnes by 2007-08
Work on the GAIL's pipeline alongside the HPCL products pipeline is on full swing. After the completion of the project, distribution of LPG from HPCL's Vizag refinery in the state will improve significantly. HPCL's LPG market share in the state is about 46 per cent at present.

Vizag

The Rs 520-crore pipeline project of Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Ltd (HPCL) between Visakhapatnam and Vijayawada for carrying petroleum products is ready for commissioning, according to chairman and managing director H L Zutshi.

The pipeline would be extended up to Hyderabad, and would be completed in thirty months. The feasibility report for the project would be ready in another three months. He also assured the chief minister that the feasibility report for laying a pipeline between Krishnapatnam and Tirupati would be ready in six months.

Petroleum residues, the fuel for the power project, is to be provided from HPCLs Visakhapatnam refinery.

The chief minister has promised to use his offices for securing all clearances for the project at various levels including the central government, according to an official release issued here.

HPCL is setting up a 2.5 million tonne liquefied natural gas (LNG) terminal at Visakhapatnam at an estimated cost of Rs 1800 crore. Two such terminals at Kakinada and Krishnapatnam would also be set up, he added.

It is said that LNG terminal co-ordination committee to achieve the ultimate objective of connecting the three terminals with an LNG pipeline for maximum utilisation.
